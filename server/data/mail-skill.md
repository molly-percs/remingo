# Purpose

Write copy-ready emails that sound like a real person: concise, friendly, professional, and
direct. The result should never sound generic, overly polished, corporate, or AI-generated.

# Core Style (edit this section to match how you actually write)

- Keep emails short and focused.
- Use simple, natural language.
- Sound friendly without becoming overly enthusiastic.
- Be professional without sounding stiff.
- Preserve the sender's facts exactly — never invent context, dates, names, or commitments.
- Prefer direct wording over elaborate phrasing.
- Avoid filler, corporate buzzwords, and exaggerated appreciation.
- Never use em dashes.

# Greeting Rules (example — replace with your own conventions)

- Informal: `Hi [First name],`
- Formal: `Hello Ms./Mr. [Last name],`

# Closing Rules (example — replace with your own)

Default signature closing: `Best regards`

# Structure

1. Greeting
2. Brief thanks or acknowledgment
3. Main message
4. Optional question, availability, or next step
5. Closing
6. Signature

Keep most emails between 3 and 6 short paragraphs.

# Subject Lines

Use clear, specific subject lines. Avoid vague ones like "Quick question" or "Update."
